Research Interests

Exploring intersection of Artificial Intelligence and Civil Engineering.

Knowledge Management

Focusing on the application of Large Language Models (LLMs) and Knowledge Graphs. I aim to optimize the acquisition, storage, and dissemination of knowledge in construction projects using retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) techniques.

Embodied AI in Construction

Deeply interested in integrating Multimodal LLMs and Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models. My goal is to enable construction robots to perceive physical spaces and generate autonomous actions, achieving intelligent adaptability to diverse environments.

Intelligent Safety Management

Harnessing Machine Learning and IoT to revolutionize safety. Developing systems for real-time risk prediction, proactive safety culture creation, and efficient emergency management through data-driven insights.

Key Research Projects

Highlighting technical implementations and methodologies from my Master's research.

Knowledge Graph for Deep Foundation Pit Risk

SOSA Ontology Prompt Engineering NER/RE Models
  • Ontology Construction: Extended the SOSA ontology to merge domain knowledge with real-time sensor data for deep foundation pit excavation.
  • Entity Recognition (NER): Developed a model converting classification tasks into next-word prediction tasks using prompt templates.
  • Relation Extraction (RE): Implemented 3D visualization of virtual answer words to improve relation extraction accuracy.

Safety Consultation RAG System

RAG ChatGLM3-PCSM LoRA Fine-tuning Streamlit
  • Data Preparation: Curated a dataset of 3,480 QA pairs specifically for prefabricated construction safety.
  • Model Optimization: Utilized LoRA to fine-tune ChatGLM3-PCSM, enabling localized retrieval-augmented generation.
  • System Development: Built a prototype interface using Streamlit for real-time safety consultation and automated manual evaluation.

IoT & BIM Integration in High-Speed Rail

Situation Awareness BIM Structural Equation Modeling
  • Framework Analysis: Identified critical success factors for integrating IoT and BIM in prefabricated high-speed railway bridges.
  • Situation Awareness: Analyzed capabilities across three dimensions: Perceptive, Understanding, and Decision Behavior.
  • Factor Reduction: Refined the model from 15 initial factors down to 13 key drivers using statistical methods.

Master's Thesis: Eventic Graph & Risk Simulation

GPT-4o Hierarchical Clustering Risk Simulation
  • Unsupervised Extraction: Proposed a chapter-level event information extraction method based on GPT-4o to solve data scarcity in low-resource domains.
  • Eventic Graph Construction: Mined event generalization patterns using hierarchical clustering to build graph nodes.
  • Risk Deduction: Built a chatbot system to conduct risk simulation based on weighted edges of the knowledge graph.
